What is FRIENDS#UNHOSTED?

FRIENDS#UNHOSTED is an alternative social network for those of us who want to own our social network data. With F#U, your data is not stored at a central server, but at a place of your choosing using an #unhosted remoteStorage. You can even host the data server on your home network, given that the server is reachable from the internet.

Signing up is a little bit different than with other social sites. Since you own your data, you need to decide where to put it, we don't do that for you. We don't own you, you do!

How do I signup?

The easiest is to get an account on one of the following storage providers:

  • 5apps

You will get a username that looks like an email address:

username@storageserver.com

Enter that username in the loginbox and press Login. You will get redirected to that storage provider site to grand FRIENDS#UNHOSTED access your storage. After granting access, you will be redirected back to FRIENDS#UNHOSTED, now in a logged in state. You can read more about #unhosted storage providers here - you can even host your own:

  • #unhosteds listing of storage providers

One of the listed services, OwnCube, is not on our list. After a serious outage and bad customer relation management, we cannot recommend this service. Feel free to use it, at your own peril.

How do I add friends?

To add a friend to your network, you need to get your friends storage address. That storage address looks like an email address:

username@storageserver.com

You add that address to your F#U friends, and then you can see the updates of your friend.

How do I find my friends?

At the moment, there is no central server where you can find your friends, that would kind of defeat the purpose of the unhosted thing. Since there is no central server, you need to get in contact with your friends using email or some existing social media. Neat, huh?!

Give me the technical details!

Regarding how #unhosted works, please read the material on unhosted.org. Simply put, it is a secure, standardized, API for storing key-value data pairs in different categories.

F#U uses (as of now in early beta) the public category storage of your unhosted account. This means that anyone can see your updates and your list of friends, in a twitterish style. The plan for the future is to share keys to lock the stuff you only want your friends to see, so stay tuned for that.

Why should I care that it is #unhosted?

The big social network sites, like Facebook, Twitter, Google and many more, gives you free storage for your stuff. But, there is no such thing as a "free lunch" (or storage). They monitor and explore your social network and your personal life to launch marketing campaigns and direct ads, and sometimes they even claim ownership of the stuff you add, like status updates, pictures and documents. If that is fine with you, then excellent, give your stuff to, and share your private life with, The Big Ones!

If, on the other hand, you have that nagging little doubt that there is something fishy going on, FRIENDS#UNHOSTED is for you!
